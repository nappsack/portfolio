# xm360

---

## Quick Facts

- **Timeframe:** April 2023 – March 2024
- **Company:** Comcast
- **Team:** 1 design lead, 5 designers, 1 copy writer, product owners, engineers
- **My Role:** Principal Experience Designer
- **Tools:** Figma, Jira, Confluence, Airtable
- **Key deliverables:** UI Kit, design documentation, multiple feature releases, process improvements

---

## Project Overview

xm360 (Xfinity Mobile 360) is the primary tool used by Xfinity retail store employees to activate and manage Xfinity Mobile customer accounts. As the only legacy product within Comcast's Frontline suite that generates revenue, xm360 is a top-priority initiative, making it one of the most fast-paced and high-stakes projects within the company. My role as Principal UX Designer involved managing the daily design operations of a four-person team while directly supporting the lead designer. Over my year on this project, I introduced structure and efficiency, improved workflows, and delivered impactful design solutions that enhanced the overall usability of xm360.

---

## Problem Statement

When I joined the XM360 team, the design process was chaotic, lacking clear sources of truth, streamlined workflows, and cohesive UI consistency. The fast-paced nature of the project meant that designers often worked in silos, leading to inconsistencies and inefficiencies in the product's experience. Additionally, there was no well-defined UI kit or centralized design documentation, making collaboration and scalability difficult.

---

## Accomplishments

Designing for XM360 required balancing efficiency, consistency, and collaboration in a fast-paced, high-stakes environment. With outdated assets, a fragmented workflow, and shifting priorities, I focused on three key areas:

1. Establishing a UI Kit and Source of Truth
2. Streamlining Workflows and Collaboration
3. Delivering High-Impact Features

Each of these initiatives helped bring structure to the design process while ensuring long-term compatibility with the Celestial Design System.

### Establishing a UI Kit and Source of Truth

**Challenges**

- XM360's design assets were outdated, and there was no structured UI documentation.
- The product would eventually transition to Celestial, requiring forward compatibility.

**Solutions**

- Developed a UI kit that standardized components, improving consistency and efficiency across the team. Created a centralized source of truth in Figma to minimize redundancy and misalignment.
- Collaborated with the Celestial team to structure assets in a way that would facilitate a smooth transition.

### Streamlining Workflows and Collaboration

**Challenges**

- The team operated in a chaotic environment with frequent pivots and shifting priorities.
- The design lead was polarizing and difficult, causing friction within the team.
- Product owners were often absent or lacked direction.

**Solutions**

- Implemented structured workflows, design review cycles, and collaborative feedback loops to ensure alignment.
- Acted as a buffer between the lead and designers, fostering trust and cohesiveness across the team.
- Took on additional responsibilities traditionally managed by product owners to ensure the project moved forward.

### Delivering High-Impact Features

**Challenges**

- The product's fast pace meant little time for iteration, leading to rushed design decisions.
- The lack of clear handoffs created miscommunication between design and engineering.

**Solutions**

- Established structured processes for validation and iteration, ensuring that design quality was upheld while meeting tight deadlines.
- Improved documentation and alignment sessions, ensuring designs were implemented accurately.

---

## Outcomes

- Standardized UI components, increasing efficiency and consistency across the design team.
- Reduced inconsistencies in XM360's UI, improving usability for retail employees.
- Strengthened collaboration between design, product, and engineering, leading to more seamless product iterations and faster development cycles.
- Established a structured transition plan for XM360's eventual migration to Celestial.
