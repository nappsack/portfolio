## Project Overview

Newell Brands, a global company managing over 300 eCommerce brands — including Marmot, Yankee Candle, and Elmer's Glue — was transitioning from Sketch and Zeplin to Figma to streamline their design and development workflows. After reading a blog post I had written on Think Company's website about migrating Comcast Business's product team to Figma, Newell reached out to enlist our expertise. This project was an exciting opportunity for Think to expand its service offerings. Over a 3-month engagement, I worked alongside a design and tech lead to train Newell's design team, migrate their existing assets, and refine their design-to-development process while helping redesign key product pages.

## Figma Training and Migration

Newell's design team needed a structured approach to transition from their fragmented workflow to a collaborative, scalable system in Figma. Our Think team — comprising two design leads, a tech lead, and a project manager — set out to understand their existing processes, pain points, and requirements before implementing the migration.

### Key Challenges

**Managing a complex design ecosystem**
Newell's "white label" design library housed unbranded templates that were later customized per brand, requiring a seamless way to manage both white-label and branded content in Figma.

**Elevating the team's Figma proficiency**
Designers needed to become confident in Figma's tools and workflows to maintain efficiency.

**Optimizing design-to-development process**
The existing workflow lacked cohesion and efficiency, leading to misalignment between teams.

### Training

After conducting research and discovery sessions, we launched an intensive training program:

**Design team training**
Over two weeks, I led daily 2-hour workshops with Newell's core design team, covering Figma's features, including frames, auto layout, and components. I also produced eight instructional videos detailing complex functionalities for ongoing reference.

**Dev team training**
Once the designers were proficient, we introduced the development team to Figma, guiding them through best practices for design handoff.

**Storybook + Figma**
Upon learning that Newell's dev team used Storybook, our tech lead identified a plugin that allowed developers to view live Figma files directly within Storybook, improving alignment between design and code.

**Migration**
To accelerate adoption, I imported foundational components (colors, typography, grid, buttons, and icons) into Figma. The design team, under our guidance, then built out the remaining components, reinforcing their learning while developing a file structure that fostered collaboration.

## UX / UI

The Newell team decided to use this opportunity to redesign their white label product catalog and product detail pages, of which they had just completed a large amount of UX research in order to determine potential layout and feature enhancements. Once the design team understood Figma at an intermediate level, we put their new skills to the test as we helped them create and deliver a redesigned version of these 2 pages. We completed the design and delivery in two 2-week sprints, and were able to develop a full end-to-end process within that timeframe.

### Product Catalog Page

The first page we worked on together was the product catalog page, which displays a list of products along with some sorting and filtering options. This involved a number of enhancements including a simplified product tile which more significantly highlights the thumbnail, along with an updated horizontal filter pattern.

### Product Detail Page

Once we delivered the product catalog page, we began working on the product detail page, focusing on emphasizing the product imagery, designing a new zoom behavior, simplifying the inputs, and enhancing the content below the fold.

## Outcomes

- Successful migration — Newell's design team fully transitioned to Figma, improving collaboration and efficiency across teams.
- Established a scalable design system — White label templates and branded content were effectively managed within Figma, ensuring consistency across hundreds of brands.
- Seamless development integration — With Storybook and Figma connected, the design-to-development handoff process was significantly improved.
- Empowered designers — The design team developed an intermediate to expert-level understanding of Figma, enabling them to maintain and evolve their design processes independently.
- Validated design workflow — The product catalog and detail page redesigns provided an immediate, tangible application of their new skills.
