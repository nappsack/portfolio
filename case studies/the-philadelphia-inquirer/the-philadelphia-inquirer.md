# The Philadelphia Inquirer

---

## Quick Facts

- **Timeframe:** July 2022 – April 2023
- **Company:** Philadelphia Inquirer
- **My Role:** Product Design Lead
- **Tools:** Figma, Storybook, Tailwind CSS, Jira, Confluence, Airtable
- **Key deliverables:** Design process development, Figma migration, design system creation, designer mentorship, CTA audit

---

## Project Overview

The Philadelphia Inquirer is one of the oldest and most respected newspapers in the United States, evolving from a traditional print publication to a modern digital news platform. As the media industry transitioned from physical newspapers to digital products, The Inquirer sought to invest heavily in user experience and product design to modernize their digital presence. In July 2022, I joined as a Product Design Lead, tasked with building a structured UX process, establishing a design system, and mentoring a team of designers to elevate the overall UX maturity of the organization.

---

## Problem Statement

The Inquirer's digital experience lacked a structured UX approach, leading to inconsistencies in design, inefficient workflows, and misalignment between design and development. The design team primarily came from graphic design and marketing backgrounds with minimal UX experience, making it difficult to implement research-backed, user-centered design. Additionally, the advertising-heavy revenue model negatively impacted the user experience, with intrusive CTAs and dark patterns disrupting content consumption. Without a systematic way to evaluate these interruptions, the product and advertising teams had little alignment on balancing business needs with usability.

---

## Process

Each initiative addressed critical gaps in design workflow, consistency, and user experience, ensuring that both the design team and cross-functional partners could operate more efficiently and collaboratively.

### Establishing a UX Process and Figma Migration

**Challenges**

- The team lacked a defined design process, leading to inconsistent workflows and inefficient handoffs.
- The team's design assets were fragmented across different tools, making collaboration difficult.

**Solutions**

- I developed a structured UX process that incorporated research, discovery, user flows, wireframes, visual design, and comprehensive delivery documentation.
- Led the migration of all design assets to Figma, ensuring a single source of truth for design work and improving collaboration.

**Impact**

- Increased efficiency, improved cross-functional communication, and established consistency across design projects.

### Building a Scalable Design System

**Challenges**

- The Inquirer lacked a cohesive design system, leading to inconsistencies in UI components and difficulties in development.
- Designers had limited experience working within a systemized approach to UI design.

**Solutions**

- Created a comprehensive design system, working closely with developers to integrate Storybook and Tailwind CSS for a seamless design-to-development workflow.
- Provided training and documentation to ensure designers understood how to leverage the design system effectively.

**Impact**

- Standardized UI components, improved developer collaboration, and reduced time spent on repetitive design tasks.

### CTA Audit and Balancing UX with Ad Revenue

**Challenges**

- The product experience was frequently disrupted by excessive CTAs and advertisements, negatively impacting user engagement.
- No clear framework existed to evaluate the severity of ad interruptions.

**Solutions**

- Partnered with the research and ad teams to conduct a full audit of all CTAs and interruptions across the product.
- Created a scoring mechanism to measure the impact of different ad types, prototyping experiences to illustrate their disruption level.

---

## Outcomes

- Successfully migrated all design assets to Figma and implemented a structured UX workflow, significantly improving efficiency and team collaboration.
- Designed and implemented a scalable design system, reducing inconsistencies and improving design-to-development handoffs.
- Established a mentorship program, providing regular 1:1s and career guidance for designers transitioning into UX roles.
- Conducted a full CTA audit, creating a formal scoring model that allowed the advertising team to consider user experience in their strategies.
- Fostered a meaningful collaboration between the product and advertising teams, bridging the gap between UX and revenue generation.

---

## Reflections

My time at The Philadelphia Inquirer was a unique challenge, as it required balancing UX best practices with business needs in an industry that relies heavily on advertising revenue. I developed critical leadership skills, from mentoring a team with limited UX experience to aligning cross-functional teams around shared goals. The experience reinforced the importance of structured design systems, thoughtful process development, and the ability to advocate for user experience in environments where business priorities may not always align. It was a rewarding opportunity to influence a major news organization's digital transformation and bridge the gap between UX and marketing in a meaningful way.
