# Xfinity Point of Sale

---

## Quick Facts

- **Timeframe:** October 2024 – Present
- **Company:** Comcast
- **Team:** 2 UX designers, 1 project manager, 1 product owner, business stakeholders, engineering partners
- **My Role:** Design Lead
- **Tools:** Figma, Jira, Confluence
- **Key deliverables:** End to end UX for POS system

---

## Project Overview

For years, Comcast's retail stores relied on XStore, a slow, buggy, and inefficient point-of-sale system outsourced by Oracle. Costing millions annually, XStore's poor design and usability issues made it a bottleneck in Comcast's retail operations. To eliminate reliance on Oracle and take control of its in-store sales experience, Comcast initiated the Point of Sale (Point of Sale) project—a ground-up product design that would align with the Celestial Design System while existing as a standalone product.

I was brought in as a Principal UX Designer due to my deep knowledge of the Celestial Design System, and within weeks, I stepped up as the Design Lead after the sudden departure of the previous design manager. Under an exceptionally aggressive timeline, I led the design team in creating an entirely new POS system, ensuring we not only met the goal of operational parody with XStore, but seizing every opportunity to vastly improve the overall user experience.

---

## Problem

XStore was an expensive, inefficient, and poorly designed third-party POS system that hindered Comcast's retail operations. With the Oracle contract renewal looming in November 2025, Comcast needed to replace XStore with an in-house solution before the deadline. However, the project faced significant roadblocks:

- Business requirements were only 20% complete
- The design team was under-resourced, and two weeks into my involvement, the design manager left, creating a leadership vacuum.

Given the aggressive timeline and lack of structure, there was a serious risk that Point of Sale would not be design-ready by the end of Q1 2025.

---

## Process

Designing and delivering Point of Sale under such a high-stakes and aggressive timeline required a structured yet adaptive approach. With business requirements incomplete and leadership gaps in place, I quickly took charge of establishing a roadmap, structuring design sprints, and ensuring alignment across product, engineering, and research teams. Our process was broken down into four key phases, each aimed at driving clarity, efficiency, and delivering a best-in-class point-of-sale experience.

### Phase 1: Establishing leadership and structure

- Stepped into the interim Design Lead role after the departure of the design manager, aligning with Frontline UX leadership to formalize my leadership position.
- Implemented sprint-based meeting cadences, including sprint planning, reviews, and structured handoffs to maintain momentum and accountability.
- Collaborated closely with the product owner to finalize business requirements and estimate a roadmap that aligned with the aggressive design timeline.

### Phase 2: Designing the POS system from the ground up

- Partnered with the other designer to build the entire Point of Sale experience from scratch, using Celestial Design System components where applicable while ensuring Point of Sale functioned as an independent product.
- Created detailed information architecture, user flows, and wireframes to define the POS experience.
- Iterated rapidly through high-fidelity visual design, improving usability and efficiency beyond XStore's baseline functionality.

### Phase 3: Integrating user research and design validation

- Brought on a UX researcher to conduct in-depth interviews (IDIs) with Celestial designers and in-store agents to validate design decisions.
- Incorporated research insights into iterative improvements, refining workflows to ensure real-world usability and alignment with retail employee needs.
- Explored Point of Sale analogs to determine industry standards and best practices.

#### Research Strategy — Our Research Goals

**Find areas for improvement**

- How intuitive do users find the prototype's interface and features? Are there any areas where they experience confusion or difficulty?
- What aspects of the prototype do users find most valuable or helpful? Which ones do they think need further refinement to enhance their overall experience?

**Identify remaining gaps**

- How can this new experience help them? Are there any other issues that need to be addressed within the experience?
- What limitations do they have if any?

### Phase 4: Executing a high-stakes, fast-paced delivery

- Worked in accelerated two-week sprints, delivering major components of the IA and UX in each cycle.
- Maintained alignment with engineering teams to ensure seamless handoff and feasibility of designs.
- On track to finish ahead of schedule, ensuring all necessary assets, documentation, and handoff materials are finalized, keeping Point of Sale on track for the development phase.

## Outcomes

- Successfully led the Point of Sale design team through an extremely tight turnaround, ensuring the project remained on schedule.
- Established a structured and efficient design process, improving communication and collaboration across design, product, and engineering.
- Delivered a high-quality, fully designed POS system that not only met the functional requirements of XStore but vastly improved upon its usability, scalability, and visual design.
- Played a pivotal role in keeping Comcast on track to eliminate their reliance on Oracle, saving millions annually in licensing costs.
- This project directly led to my promotion to Design Lead at Think Company in January 2025.
